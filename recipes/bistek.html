Bistek Recipe

Description

Usually eaten with steamed white rice, bistek, or beef steak, is a delicious, tangy, and salty beef dish from the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ribeye or skirt steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 sweet yellow onions, sliced into rings
  • 2 medium potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, thinly sliced into rounds
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsps Worcestershire sauce
  • 3 tbsps calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

For the Marinade

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/3 cup calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 whole garlic, cloved and crushed
  • 3/4 tsp ground black pepper

For the Cornstarch Slurry

  • 1 1/2 tbsps cornstarch
  • 1 1/2 tbsps cold water
  • Mix the cornstach and water together until a paste forms.

Steps

  1. Mix the marinade ingredients and add the sliced beef in a large Pyrex bowl, making sure the marinade is well incorporated into all the pieces. Cover with plastic wrap and place in the refrigerator overnight or for at least 6 hours.
  2. Pan fry the onions, potatoes, and carrots separately. Set aside.
  3. Remove the beef from the marinade. Using the same pan as above, sear the beef slices for 30 seconds per side. Set aside.
  4. Saute the minced garlic. Add the marinade, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, calamansi or lemon juice, water, and ground black pepper and bring to a boil. Add the cornstarch slurry to thicken. You can add more cornstarch slurry if you want a thicker sauce (ratio is 1:1 of cornstarch and cold water).
  5. Add the beef back in and let simmer for a couple of minutes.
  6. Serve with steamed white rice and the pan-fried vegetables.
